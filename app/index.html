Project Re-Animator
Postcard One
01 June 2015

ORD > SFO > MUC > SFO

Hello, beloved chIDEO fam'!

It has been a pretty intense first month of travel & being brought up to speed on the state of the project. That said, I'm finally getting settled, so I figured I would check in with a few postcard-worthy highlights.

I arrived in SF on Friday, May 1st. I'm staying in The Haight neighborhood, ever adjacent neo-hippie panhandlers and immediately across the street from the stunningly beautiful Buena Vista Park.

MarCom certainly did not skimp on the Airbnb; the view from my living room is about as epic as San Francisco sights come:

Room with a view: Lower Haight, Tenderloin, Financial District, SoMa, Mission, Portero Hill & Dogpatch.

My Reputation Precedes Me.

Thanks to Anet & Scott, I arrived to a welcoming assemblage of supplies to assist me in settling in at IDEO SF. When asked why these items awaited me, I was forced to explain my ritual bourbon aging using only tupperware containers and ziploc sandwich bags.

Don't Get Too Settled …

After a few days in the office and enough time to procure what remaining necessities I had not found on my desk, our team was off to Munich for the website handoff from IDEO GmbH.

Deutschland über alles

The SF & Munich teams formed like voltron and holed up in our German studio's ‘attic’ for an intense week of design sprints, planning and coding. Those yankee among us were surprised to enjoy quite a lot of particularly good Thai food, the Munich team's go-to favorite for lunchtime. (Seriously, if you're ever in our Munich office, Manam Thai is no joke. Trust me on this one.)

The Team [L to R]: Chris Nyffler, Marcus Hauer, Lorenzo Fernandez, Christi Ginger, Marcus Paeschke, Jane Ha, Stephan Seidt, Jure Martinec (not pictured: me.)

Munich's River Surfers

One of the city's must-see highlights is the river surfers on the Eisbach. What you assume will be a bit of a joke is anything but. These men and women are incredibly skilled, and they're honing their talents under some pretty dangerous conditions.

(Big hat tip to Jessica for reminding me to check them out!)

MLM, IDEO Munich-Style

You may notice some differenes in the food Munich serves realtive to the typical chIDEO MLM fare. Matjes Herring Salad is — I am told — a tradiaional German dish consisting of marinated herring, apples, white onions and other such … whathaveyou … all marinating in a bath of vaguely creamy yogurt stuffs. (Truth be told, it was actually quite delicious, as though in spite of its vulgar appearance.)

Not entirely immune to a millennial class of photographic vanity, I snapped a rare selfie at the Siegstor (Victory Gate) before bidding München Auf Wiedersehen.

After sustaining heavy damage in World War II, the gate was reconstructed and restored only partially. Its new inscription reads, “Dedicated to victory, destroyed by war, urging peace.”
#AlwaysReadThePlaque

I'm going going, back back …

It has been a busy few weeks getitng settled and popping over to the Munich studio for the project handoff, but I'm back in SF and jamming on what I've lovingly dubbed, ‘Project Re-Animator.’

That's all for now. Should any of you find yourselves in SF this summer, oysters & Anchor Lagers are on me.

Miss you guys!
